Tips

Empty files are typically generated by jobs, such as _SUCCESS or _DONE files.
In the case of most job-created files you can simply delete them.
If your job continues to output many empty part files it is likely you have over-tuned your job; consider reducing your mappers and reducers.
In the event that your application uses these files, it is recommended you instead find an alternate way to record the event rather than as an empty file.
One example is to keep a log and append to it instead.

Tips

Be very careful with generating too many tiny files!
These are a great source of burden on the NameNode and DataNodes during balancing.
If you have old datasets with tiny files it is recommended to HAR or GZIP the directories containing them.
You may wish to analyze your datasets and make sure they are not just compressed empty files with compression headers.
Pig, Hive, and MapReduce have support for reading HARs (Hadoop Archives).
You should also consider tuning your mappers or reducers, or adding a reduce phase to minimize the number of final files.

Tips

Be very careful with generating too many of these files!
These are a great source of burden on the NameNode and DataNodes during balancing.
Unlike tiny files, small files typically come in large quantities from jobs and ingest.
You should consider tuning your mappers or reducers, or adding a reduce phase to minimize the number of final files if possible.
In most cases a post-cleanup job that compacts and archives will be necessary.

Tips

Your cluster administrators may have set quotas on your directories.
In most cases all you need to do is observe you are not close to your quota limit.
If you are close to your namespace limit you can remedy by reducing your file count.
If you are close to your diskspace limit you can remedy by deleting large or old files.
Another consideration is to ask for your quota limits to be raised.
